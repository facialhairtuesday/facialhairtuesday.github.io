Marc Stiller

DARPA Transformer (TX)

Lead Engineer and Project Manager

Terrafugia Sub-Contract - Phase II

The TX concept centered on developing a fully autonomous VTOL aircraft that could be used to transport troops and cargon. Terrafugia's role was to develop a 3/4-scale static ground-test prototype and evaluate the performance of ducted fan thrust and control surface effectiveness. I led a team of 15 engineers and technicians through concept generation, construction, assembly, inspection, and testing. Had weekly check-in with contract prime, including schedule and performace reviews. Performed all design work for vehicle body, including outer mold line CAD scaling/model modifications, construction method development for body and fan duct, and sub-system integration and mounting.

NASA Propfan

3/4-scale ground test vehicle based around NASA model of ducted-fan personal air vehicle. Trailer contained mounted model with power train and outer-mold-line from NASA vehicle.

Concept CAD Development

CAD model developed to include TX outer mold line, fan ducting, and control surfaces. Significant modifications were required, including major changes to structural mounting, design and incorporation of TX outer mold line, new duct shape, power train mounting, and sensor integration.

Body Construction - Initial Steps

Designed and developed a quickly actionable method of construction for the body. Lightweight foam assembled into rough body shape, then CNC-milled to near-net shape.

Body Construction - Intermediate Steps

Foam reinforced with fiberglass, then covered in tooling paste.

Body Construction - Final Steps

Tooling paste CNC milled to final body shape, ready for finishing.

Fan Duct Mold Creation

Fan duct required a significantly higher precision surface than body. Tightly-toleranced mold CNC-milled from tooling foam.

Inner Shell Layup - Fan Duct

Fiberglass RTM technique used to create high-quality inner duct surface from mold.

Fan Duct Reinforcement

Fan duct reinforced with aluminum and foam structure, covered in fiberglass shell. Outer surface of duct also coated with tooling paste and milled to final shape.

Initial Assembly

Test assembly of completed body and duct pieces onto structrue.

Final Inspection

Part inspection and verification completed at multiple stages during development.

Performance Testing

Multiple engine run tests performed, with full data-logging.
