Transition Front Suspension

Lead engineer for Transition landing gear/suspension systems. Designed a robust, light weight system, capable of handling all driving/flight/landing loads and meeting all regulatory requirements from a variety of governing bodies (FAA/ASTM/FMVSS), all while fitting in an extremely limited space envelope.

Fully independent, double A-arm suspension with pull-rod actuated custom shock/damper.

Requirements Generation

Complete set of requirements generated to cover regulatory compliance issues, performance goals, passenger comfort, and vehicle dynamics.

Basic Hand Calcs

Analyzed multiple design parameters, starting with hand calculations to determine rough minimum component sizing, vehicle structural requirements, and performance response.

Basic Kinematic Checks

Simplified wireframe kinematic model generated to allow for testing performance characteristics of suspension during various vehicle events (landing, driving, turns, etc.). Completed analysis of vehicle dynamics with multiple iterations of suspension geometry locations to dial in suspension geometry.

Initial CAD and Analysis

Generated first pass CAD models for all components and ran rough analysis for initial evaluation.

Detailed CAD Design

Detail added to CAD models, including all COTS components.

Final Analysis

Detailed final analysis completed for all parts, including final load scenarios and optimized parts. Structural and component fatigue analysis also completed.

Final CAD Assembly

All components added to CAD model to verify kinematics, structural interactions/mounting, and installation plan.
